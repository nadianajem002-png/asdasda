üíä Treatment Quick Reference

GERD Treatment:
  • Lifestyle: avoid large meals, alcohol, smoking; elevate head; weight loss
  • Meds: Antacids (symptom relief), PPIs (acid suppression)
Peptic Ulcer Treatment:
  • Eradicate H. pylori (antibiotics + PPI)
  • Stop NSAIDs
  • PPIs, antacids, mucosal protectants
Hepatitis C Treatment:
  • Direct-acting antivirals (DAA) - >90% cure rate!
  • Block virus replication
Cirrhosis Treatment:
  • Cannot cure - transplant best option
  • Supportive: albumin (edema/ascites), paracentesis, clotting factors, antibiotics (encephalopathy)
IBD Treatment:
  • Lifestyle: smoking cessation (Crohn's), diet, exercise
  • Antibiotics (control flora)
  • Immunosuppression (methotrexate)
  • Anti-inflammatories (steroids)
  • Surgery if needed (fistulas, obstructions, resections)

üß¨ Mechanisms to Understand

Liver Drug Metabolism (Phase 1 & 2)

Phase 1: The liver chemically changes a drug (often makes it less active). This mainly happens in the smooth ER.

  • Alcohol can change how fast drugs are broken down ‚Üí some drugs wear off faster or become more toxic.

Phase 2: The liver ‚Äúpackages‚Äù the drug/toxins to make them water-soluble so you can pee/poop them out.

  • Glutathione helps neutralize toxins (important for acetaminophen).
  • Chronic alcohol can lower glutathione ‚Üí acetaminophen becomes more dangerous.
  • N-acetylcysteine (NAC) helps rebuild glutathione (antidote early).
Alcohol Metabolism & Liver Damage
  • Alcohol ‚Üí acetaldehyde (toxic) + free radicals ‚Üí inflammation/scarring over time.
  • Progression: fatty liver ‚Üí alcoholic hepatitis ‚Üí cirrhosis.
Gastric Mucosal Barrier Protection
  • The stomach protects itself using mucus + bicarbonate + good blood flow.
  • NSAIDs/aspirin reduce prostaglandins ‚Üí less mucus/bicarb ‚Üí higher ulcer risk.
Pancreatic Enzyme Safety Mechanism
  • Pancreatic enzymes are stored ‚Äúoff‚Äù (inactive) and turn ‚Äúon‚Äù in the duodenum.
  • In acute pancreatitis, they turn on too early inside the pancreas ‚Üí self-digestion.
Portal Hypertension Mechanism
  • Scarred liver blocks blood flow ‚Üí pressure backs up in portal vein.
  • Blood finds detours ‚Üí varices; fluid shifts into belly ‚Üí ascites.
