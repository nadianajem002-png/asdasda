üî¨ Diagnostic Tests Quick Guide

Liver Function Tests:
  • ALT/AST: ‚Üë = liver cell injury (transamination)
  • Bilirubin, GGT, Alk Phos: Hepatic excretory function of bile

üíä Treatment Quick Reference

GERD Treatment:
  • Lifestyle: avoid large meals, alcohol, smoking; elevate head; weight loss
  • Meds: Antacids (symptom relief), PPIs (acid suppression)
Peptic Ulcer Treatment:
  • Eradicate H. pylori (antibiotics + PPI)
  • Stop NSAIDs
  • PPIs, antacids, mucosal protectants
Hepatitis C Treatment:
  • Direct-acting antivirals (DAA) - >90% cure rate!
  • Block virus replication
Cirrhosis Treatment:
  • Cannot cure - transplant best option
  • Supportive: albumin (edema/ascites), paracentesis, clotting factors, antibiotics (encephalopathy)
IBD Treatment:
  • Lifestyle: smoking cessation (Crohn's), diet, exercise
  • Antibiotics (control flora)
  • Immunosuppression (methotrexate)
  • Anti-inflammatories (steroids)
  • Surgery if needed (fistulas, obstructions, resections)

üß¨ Mechanisms to Understand

Liver Drug Metabolism (Phase 1 & 2)

Phase 1: Chemical modification/inactivation (microsomal enzymes in smooth ER)

  • Alcohol can accelerate drug metabolism ‚Üí ‚Üì half-life

Phase 2: Lipid-soluble ‚Üí water-soluble derivatives

  • Glutathione conjugates toxic products (e.g., Tylenol/acetaminophen)
  • Glutathione depleted during detox
  • Chronic alcohol depletes glutathione ‚Üí Tylenol becomes VERY toxic
  • Treatment: N-acetylcysteine (NAC) - replenishes glutathione
Alcohol Metabolism & Liver Damage
  • ADH system: Alcohol ‚Üí acetaldehyde (TOXIC) + free radicals
  • Generates excess NADH ‚Üí liver damage
  • Alcohol competes for NAD ‚Üí ‚Üë lactic acid
  • ‚Üì NAD ‚Üí impairs gluconeogenesis
  • MEOS pathway: Smooth ER - induces enzymes ‚Üí ‚Üë toxicity
  • Progression: Fatty liver ‚Üí alcoholic hepatitis ‚Üí cirrhosis
Gastric Mucosal Barrier Protection
  • Impermeable epithelial cells with tight junctions
  • Hydrophobic mucus/lipid covering
  • Bicarbonate layer (alkaline interface)
  • Mucus protects from pepsin digestion + provides lubrication
  • Aspirin: Non-ionized/lipid-soluble ‚Üí passes through, damages epithelium, ‚Üì mucus (inhibits prostaglandins)
  • Prostaglandins: Inhibit gastrin, stimulate bicarbonate + mucus, ‚Üë blood flow
Pancreatic Enzyme Safety Mechanism
  • Normal: Enzymes secreted INACTIVE (zymogens)
  • Trypsinogen, chymotrypsinogen, etc.
  • Activated in DUODENUM (not pancreas)
  • Acute pancreatitis: Premature activation INSIDE pancreas ‚Üí AUTODIGESTION
Portal Hypertension Mechanism
  • Cirrhotic liver = obstruction to blood flow
  • ‚Üë Resistance ‚Üí ‚Üë back pressure in portal vein
  • Blood seeks alternate routes ‚Üí collateral channels (varices)
  • ‚Üì Albumin + ‚Üë pressure ‚Üí fluid shifts ‚Üí ascites
  • Spleen congestion ‚Üí splenomegaly
